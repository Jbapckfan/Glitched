Level 7 — Screenshot (Side + Vol-Up) freezes a flickering ghost bridge solid for a degrading window (5/3.5/2/1s) so Bit can cross an un-jumpable chasm.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The freeze-to-cross loop is legible and the geometry is honest: the 220pt chasm is correctly wider than Bit's ~184pt reach, so the bridge is genuinely required, and the degrading-freeze + screenshotCount-reset-on-death + 0.2s coyote grace make repeated attempts fair rather than punishing. The 4th-wall "that's in your camera roll forever" beat lands perfectly for the theme.
CONCERNS: Two near-identical panels both lead with "SOME MOMENTS REFUSE TO MOVE..." (the t=0 discovery panel AND the persistent instruction panel) — redundant and it reads like a duplicate-render bug. On iPad the composed climb fills the floor well (no void/overlap at the platforms), but the entire upper-center canvas is dead white with a lone crosshair, and the finale chasm/exit scroll off-screen right, so at t=0 the player can't see where they're going. The mechanic also relies on a real OS gesture that fires an actual system screenshot each use — unavoidable side effect, but worth confirming it never gets debounced/swallowed by the screenshot UI.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 is well-judged: "THE BRIDGE ONLY EXISTS WHEN NOTHING IS LOOKING. SO LOOK. / CATCH IT BEFORE IT FORGETS ITSELF." teases the freeze-the-moment idea without naming the screenshot gesture. Escalation is real: notePlayerStruggle() fires on every chasm death and hintText() at the base ~18s delay spells out "press Side + Volume Up," so a stuck player gets the explicit reveal.
SUGGESTION: Cut the redundant second panel: keep the atmospheric t=0 discovery line OR the instruction panel, not both with the same opening sentence. And on iPad, either widen the camera or seed a faint "ghost preview" of the finale bridge so the empty mid-canvas isn't dead space at spawn.
