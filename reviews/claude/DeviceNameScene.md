Level 21 — Name-door: the OS reads your device name, greets you by it, and the exit is the one door (of three, flanked by GUEST/ADMIN decoys) labeled with your name; a doppelganger NPC races you and dies at a decoy.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Strong fourth-wall hook — being addressed by your real device name on a labeled door lands. iPad path is excellent: the centered low band is replaced by a hand-paced full-height zig-zag climb with the door cluster at the ceiling, so there is no void and the screen fills top-to-bottom. Bay pillars cleverly force a real choice instead of a single rightward stroll.
CONCERNS: Once names resolve the "puzzle" is trivial and consequence-free — wrong doors are just inert, so you walk to your name with no failure cost, making the doppelganger race largely decorative/redundant with the labels. On iPhone the t=0 narrator line "I KNOW WHO YOU ARE..." renders mid-band and overlaps the astronaut and platforms (legible but cluttered); doors show only "?" for the first ~5s until the name event/fallback fires, which can read as a stall. Jump-reach is fine (all gaps ~5-20pt, single 30pt rise, far inside 130/85).
CLUE/HINT: Yes — t=0 "FIND THE DOOR WITH YOUR NAME" + "THE DOOR KNOWS TOO" points at the name mechanic without spoiling the doppelganger trick; and notePlayerStruggle()/hintText() surfaces "Your device knows your name..." after repeated deaths, so a stuck player gets escalating, non-spoiling help.
SUGGESTION: Give the wrong doors a real consequence so the choice has stakes: make a decoy door visibly "reject"/flash-and-respawn (or briefly lock) on contact, and arm the doppelganger to actually beat you to the exit if you dawdle — turn the labeled-name read into a timed decision rather than a labeled walk.
