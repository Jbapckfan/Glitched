Level 31 — Flashlight + device-tilt to reveal a dark cave: an SKCropNode masks all level content to an ellipse beam; held vertical the beam throws long/forward to spot ceiling stalactites, held flat it pools at the feet to catch pit gaps.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The crop-mask beam that morphs shape with pitch (vertical=long forward throw, flat=floor pool) is a genuinely clever, on-theme use of hardware, and the always-on ambient glow + faintly pulsing exit glow are smart fail-safes so you can never be fully blind. iPhone gaps all sit safely (largest Section 3/4 gap ~60pt vs 130 cap) and Section 4 stalactites were deliberately raised to gapFromFloor 175 to clear the jump apex, so it is completable. The de-spoiled t=0 tease and the strong escalating-hint chain are the high points.
CONCERNS: Discovery of the core trick is gated behind 2 deaths + 8s, a long blind window; on iPad the unlit canvas reads as an empty void because silhouette hints sit at ~0.04 alpha. Geometry/completability are solid on both form factors.
CLUE/HINT: Good on both axes: t=0 panel ("IT'S DARK DOWN HERE. I CAN'T SEE A THING FROM IN HERE — CAN YOU?") teases without naming the flashlight trick, and a stuck player escalates from per-death narrator whispers to the explicit hintText() ("Switch on the phone's flashlight. Stand the phone upright to throw the beam far ahead — tilt it flat to pool the light...") after struggleCount>=2 and >=8s.
SUGGESTION: Make the discovery window shorter and self-serve: if the flashlight is still OFF after ~6-8s of no progress (not gated behind 2 deaths), surface a soft mid-tier nudge that points at the device's flashlight control without the full solution — e.g. animate/pulse the on-screen flashlight HUD button. Separately, raise the iPad silhouette-hint alpha to ~0.10-0.12 so the dark reads as an intentional cave rather than a void even before the beam is on.
