Level 5 — Plug your real device into a charger; a giant plug bursts through the floor and elevators Bit up the shaft to the exit (.deviceCharging input -> triggerPlugAnimation, per-frame carry).
VERDICT: polish (3/5)
STRENGTHS: The carry/softlock engineering is genuinely robust: per-frame deltaY carry covers both the scripted rise and post-arrival sink, the respawn is relocated onto the exit platform after the floor breaks, plugMaxSink keeps a near-floor unplug recoverable, and the unplug/replug "COLD. SO COLD." loop is a nice fourth-wall beat. iPad layout fills the full canvas with no void.
CONCERNS: The whole conceit is a fourth-wall "do something physical" surprise, yet a permanent on-screen label literally reads PLUG IN YOUR CHARGER, plus a pulsing empty-battery icon, so the trick is fully spoiled at t=0 with zero discovery moment. iPad-only adds a long gutter-climb/switchback that the iPhone never has, so the two devices play as different levels; the central vertical junction-box bead columns flanking the battery read as climbable but carry no physics.
CLUE/HINT: Fails the t=0 test: the literal solution (PLUG IN YOUR CHARGER) and hintText() both state the answer outright from frame one. There is no notePlayerStruggle override and no escalating reveal — help is binary and always-on rather than graduated.
SUGGESTION: Stage the clue over time instead of spoiling it: at t=0 show only the dying/pulsing empty battery + a vague glitch taunt (e.g. "I'M... FADING"), and surface the explicit "Connect your device to a charger" string only after ~15-20s of inactivity via notePlayerStruggle/hintText, so a stuck player still gets the answer but a fresh player gets the aha.
