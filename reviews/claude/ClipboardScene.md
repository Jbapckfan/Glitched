Level 12 — Copy the password GLITCH3D to the iOS clipboard outside the level, return, and tap PASTE PASSWORD on the terminal to unlock the locked door before the exit.
VERDICT: polish (3/5)
STRENGTHS: Genuinely inventive fourth-wall clipboard mechanic with a well-built un-jumpable locked-door trap (145pt door body puts the top ~16.5pt above Bit's ~91pt apex) and clean user-initiated paste that dodges the iOS paste prompt. iPhone layout is crisp: redacted password field, clear PASTE PASSWORD button, and an atmospheric two-line clue all legible.
CONCERNS: The core fairness gap: the literal answer GLITCH3D appears ONLY in hintText() — it is redacted on the terminal and nothing in-scene copies it to the pasteboard, so a first-timer cannot know what to copy without the hint. On iPad the entire signature finale (terminal, PASTE control, redacted field, clue) is staged near the top tier and is OFF-SCREEN to the right at t=0; only blank platform stubs and the clipboard FAB are visible, so the puzzle reads as a staircase to nowhere until the camera scrolls.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 clue ("IT REMEMBERS WHAT YOU LAST HELD." / "...SOMEWHERE YOU ALREADY WERE.") is nicely atmospheric and does not spoil. Escalation exists via notePlayerStruggle()/notePlayerProgress()/hintText(), but struggle only increments on death — a player stuck by confusion (standing, not dying) never triggers escalation, the exact failure mode this clipboard puzzle produces.
SUGGESTION: Make the answer discoverable in-world and tie escalation to confusion, not just death: render the literal string GLITCH3D somewhere copyable in-scene (e.g. a "select to copy" glyph/log line near the terminal, or seed it onto the pasteboard with a visible "COPIED: GLITCH3D" toast) so the puzzle is solvable without reading hintText, and add a timed/idle notePlayerStruggle() trigger so a stuck-but-not-dying player still earns the escalating hint.
