Level 28 — Share a scrambled 6-symbol transmission through the system share sheet (AirDrop/Messages/Notes) to "decode" it (payload is the plaintext), then type the 6 symbols back on a decoy-salted keypad to unlock an un-jumpable door.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Genuinely clever diegetic decode: sending the cipher out of the app and reading the plaintext back is a real, novel use of the share sheet, and the salted keypad means you must actually know the answer rather than mash keys. iPhone terminal layout is clean and self-explanatory, and the door-height gate (120pt body vs ~91pt apex) correctly forces the puzzle.
CONCERNS: The core loop depends on the OS share sheet round-trip, which is fragile/opaque on first encounter — a player can complete the share without understanding that the *received text* is the answer, especially if they share to Notes and don't switch apps. The visible "RULE: ROTATE BACK 5" plus the 6-char cipher invites players to try decoding in-head, but the keypad's symbol set is restricted, so manual rotation may not even be tappable, which is confusing. On iPad the finale (terminal + door) is fully off-screen at spawn; the screenshot is a long empty staircase with no visible goal, so the signature mechanic isn't previewed at all.
CLUE/HINT: Good: t=0 ("THE CODE IS SCRAMBLED / I CAN'T READ MYSELF IN HERE / TAKE ME SOMEWHERE I CAN") nudges toward sharing without naming AirDrop or spoiling that the payload is the plaintext. Escalation is solid: hintText() gives a full step-by-step, notePlayerStruggle() fires on wrong submits AND deaths, and the "CAN'T DO THIS?" fallback reveals the decoded code on the terminal yet still requires typing it.
SUGGESTION: On iPad, anchor a small persistent "TRANSMISSION AHEAD →" arrow or a camera-pinned mini-preview of the locked door so the scrolled finale is telegraphed from spawn; the empty staircase currently hides the entire mechanic. Secondarily, drop or de-emphasize the literal "RULE: ROTATE BACK 5" line since it baits unsolvable in-head decoding and undercuts the share gimmick.
