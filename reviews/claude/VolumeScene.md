Level 4 — Hardware volume buttons — lower the volume to keep the wolf asleep and the flood water low while walking to the exit.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Strong, legible single-screen staging: sleeping wolf with Z's, a dashed detection radius, and a live volume HUD make the cause-and-effect readable. The state machine (sleeping/stirring/awake/return-to-sleep) plus error haptic and the volume->water flood give the input two reinforcing consequences, and the de-spoiled poetic t=0 text fits the game's tone.
CONCERNS: First-encounter fairness is the weak point: the input is a physical button with NO on-screen control, and the top-right volume widget is a passive readout that can read as decorative, so a new player may not connect "keep it quiet" to the hardware rocker. On iPad isWideCanvas is hardcoded false, so the whole hand-composed vertical-climb path is dead code and the level runs the flat phone band lifted to mid-screen — leaving a large empty void in the bottom ~40% (only faint waves) and a sparse top, so it does not fill the iPad screen.
CLUE/HINT: Yes on both counts but with a hole: t=0 ("IT SLEEPS. THE WATER LISTENS.") teaches the goal without naming the volume button, and notePlayerStruggle on each death escalates to hintText which explicitly says "Press your device's Volume-Down button." The gap: the escalation only fires on death (wolf/drown/fall), so a player who is simply stuck — standing still, never raising volume, never nearing the wolf — never triggers a struggle and never earns the reveal.
SUGGESTION: Trigger the struggle/hint escalation on idle-stuck (no exit progress after ~15s), not only on death, so a passive stuck player still gets the volume-button reveal; and animate the volume HUD bars (a brief pulse/finger-tap cue at t=0) so it reads as the interactive control rather than a passive meter.
