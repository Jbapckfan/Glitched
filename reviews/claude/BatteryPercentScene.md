Level 23 — Battery % controls how many stones exist; the real exit is hidden below the 50% stone and only unlocks when battery drops under 60% (so you must DRAIN power, not keep it).
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The counterintuitive "drain to win" trick is genuinely clever and is implemented airtight: the hidden exit body starts inert (category .none) and only becomes a real .exit at <60%, so it can't be cheesed at full battery. The DRAIN POWER fallback makes it playable in sim, dim-overlay + atmosphere mood shift give satisfying drain feedback, and the iPad climb maps % to altitude so draining visibly drops the top of the course away.
CONCERNS: First-encounter fairness is shaky: nothing tells the player the DRAIN POWER button is the answer, so the intended insight (lower battery to reveal the exit) is easy to miss while they bounce off the fake EXIT? trap. On iPad the 40% stone's right edge (~612) overlaps the 50% plateau's left edge (~570), a ~43pt overlap, and a wide unlabeled connective platform sits ambiguously next to the plateau; vertical fill is otherwise good (no void). Reach is fine — start->10% gap ~50pt, tier rises sized by fillTierCount so each +1 step stays <=85.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 text ("SOME THINGS ONLY APPEAR WHEN YOU'RE RUNNING LOW / MORE POWER ISN'T ALWAYS BETTER") telegraphs the inversion without naming battery or the drain action, which is the right amount of misdirection. Escalation works via notePlayerStruggle()->hintText "Lower your battery % — the real way down only appears when power drops," but it's death-gated, so a player who merely wanders or only hits the fake exit may never trigger it.
SUGGESTION: Make struggle detection time/idle-based as well as death-based (e.g. start the hint timer after N seconds with battery still >=60% or after the player has touched the fake exit twice), so a stuck-but-not-dying player still gets escalated toward DRAIN POWER. Separately, nudge the 40% stone left or the 50% plateau right to remove the ~43pt iPad overlap.
