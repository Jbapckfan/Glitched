Level 8 — Toggle system dark/light to swap which ghost platforms are solid (moon=dark, sun=light); the exit door only unlocks in dark mode, while a shadow enemy makes lingering in dark on the light platform a hazard.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The "real platforms are a rumor in the other mode" 4th-wall conceit is genuinely clever and the dashed-ghost + moon/sun icons read clearly. Layout math is disciplined: an always-solid rest ledge guarantees Bit never stands on a vanishing platform, and the door multiplier was deliberately raised to 3.55 to close a rest->door bypass, so the required flip is truly un-skippable. Jump geometry is safe (rises ~25-57pt, well under 85; adjacent platforms overlap horizontally on iPhone so gaps are near-zero).
CONCERNS: The core action is invisible: nothing on screen tells the player the trick is the OS Settings/Control-Center dark-mode switch, not an in-game button — and the prominent "TOGGLE DARK MODE" button + sun/moon pills only appear via the accessibility fallback (needsFallbackUI), so most players see a clue panel pointing at a control they may not realize is the system toggle. The iPad screenshot shows the upper ~60% of the canvas as empty void at this camera position with the climb hugging the bottom; the full-height composed climb only fills the screen as the camera pans, so the opening frame reads as a layout void. Tiny 7-8pt Menlo clue text is borderline illegible and partly occluded by the phone icon.
CLUE/HINT: Yes on both. The t=0 panel ("NIGHT AND DAY DON'T AGREE ON WHAT'S REAL... THE DOOR ONLY TRUSTS THE DARK") hints the day/night duality and dark-locked door without naming the action. Escalation is wired correctly: notePlayerStruggle() on each death progressively surfaces the explicit hintText ("Swipe down from the top-right and tap Dark Mode... the exit only unlocks in dark mode"), and notePlayerProgress() resets it once the player enters dark and unlocks the door.
SUGGESTION: Make the system-toggle action discoverable without forcing a death-loop: after ~6-8s of no appearance change, auto-present a one-line GlitchedNarrator nudge that explicitly mimes the Control-Center swipe gesture (an animated finger/arrow from the top-right), so a first-timer learns the mechanic is the OS switch before frustration — distinct from the earned full hintText that still waits on real struggle.
