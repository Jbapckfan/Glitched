Level 33 — Flat single-screen finale: walk right through two self-opening joke gates to a padlocked EXIT; the review prompt padlock auto-breaks after 10s whether or not you tap "VALIDATE ME."
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The gag lands: t=0 promises "JUST GET TO THE EXIT" while the real puzzle is the fourth-wall review ask, and the level can never soft-lock because the padlock auto-breaks after 10s. Completability is bulletproof on iPhone: it is a pure flat walk with zero jumps, gaps, or rises, and the gates open on contact rather than gating behind a jump.
CONCERNS: iPad framing is the main issue: gameplayLift centers the band but leaves the entire lower ~40% of the canvas as empty dotted void (decor only brackets the upper band, nothing fills below the ground line). The gates also lose legibility on iPad, reading as thin full-height white poles fused with the new pillars rather than two distinct gate uprights, so the "gate" gag is much weaker than on iPhone.
CLUE/HINT: Good: the t=0 panel ("HOW HARD CAN IT BE?") sets up the joke without exposing it, and hintText() escalates to "The game wants something from you..." pointing at the review ask without spelling it out. But there is no notePlayerStruggle hook, and the 10s auto-unlock makes the hint nearly moot since a stuck player is rescued automatically.
SUGGESTION: On iPad, anchor the flat band lower (reduce gameplayLift so the ground sits near the lower third) and/or extend the decorative shaft structure below the ground line, so the empty bottom void is eliminated; while there, give the two gates a distinct cap/base so they don't visually merge with the new vertical pillars.
