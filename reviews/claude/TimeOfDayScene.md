Level 25 — Real-clock time-of-day: NIGHT sleeps the patrolling spike guarding the only safe landing; CYCLE TIME button overrides day/night/secret(3:33) on demand.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The required-mechanic loop is genuinely sound: the DAY guard sweeps P2's only safe landing so the player must switch to NIGHT to sleep it, and the override button decouples the puzzle from the real clock so it is always solvable. Geometry checks out on phone (P1->P2 open gap 130 <= reach, all rises <= ~13pt) and the secret 3:33 variant is a nice meta payoff.
CONCERNS: On the iPhone shot the two guard spikes sit on the LEFT shoulder of the wide P2/exit pad rather than centered over the forced landing zone, so a player may read "land right of the spikes" and never feel forced to use NIGHT (the puzzle risks reading as a timing/positioning dodge rather than a required toggle). The P1->P2 single jump is also the only real challenge, so the level is very short. iPad layout fills the full height correctly with no void/overlap and the HUD (time/cycle button) is camera-pinned.
CLUE/HINT: Good: the t=0 panel ("THE WORLD CHANGES WITH THE CLOCK" / "SOMETHING GUARDS THAT LEDGE. IT WON'T ALWAYS BE WATCHING.") points at the day/night idea without naming NIGHT or the button, so the trick is not spoiled. Escalation also works: handleDeath() calls notePlayerStruggle() and hintText() explicitly says tap CYCLE TIME until it reads NIGHT, so repeated deaths surface real help.
SUGGESTION: Re-center the guard's patrol so its sweep fully covers P2's ONLY safe landing band (extend enemy 0 range or shift center so the awake DAY spike makes every viable landing lethal), and make the spike visually overlap the platform top edge so a first-time player clearly cannot land there in DAY.
