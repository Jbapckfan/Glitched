Level 10 — Rotate device to landscape to disarm a horizontally-creeping crusher and widen a too-narrow corridor (gap 18->100pt) so flat Bit can walk through to the exit. No jumps.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Strong "show don't tell" reveal: a looping 90-degree phone-rotation icon + circular arrow + top stretch-arrows + the 18pt gap vs 22pt Bit make the answer discoverable without naming the trick. The first-life 5s near-idle creep grace + 2s respawn grace turn what was a ~2.5s insta-kill into a fair, readable first encounter, and notePlayerStruggle escalates to an explicit hintText reveal after repeated death.
CONCERNS: iPad portrait wastes a lot of canvas: the flat course is marooned in the lower-left and roughly the upper half plus right third read as empty grid despite the pillar/chevron framing, so it does not truly fill the screen (no overlap, but a visible void). On iPhone the crusher/skull is clipped off the left edge and two stacked top-center HUD plates crowd the header band. As a free-demo CLOSER this is the only level whose solution lives outside the touchscreen, so a player who never thinks to physically rotate can stall right at the paywall.
CLUE/HINT: Yes on both: t=0 lines ("UP IS A MATTER OF OPINION...", "THIS HALL WASN'T / BUILT FOR YOU.") tease hostile geometry without ever saying "rotate," and the animated rotation icon implies it visually; a stuck player gets notePlayerStruggle escalation per death plus the explicit hintText "Rotate the device to landscape... the corridor widens." Escalation is solid.
SUGGESTION: Tighten the iPad framing: instead of the bottom-left pin, scale/center the flat course so the floor sits mid-screen and the camera band fills width edge-to-edge, and on iPhone shift the world right so the crusher/skull is fully on-screen (it is the visual that motivates fleeing right). Also consider auto-surfacing the rotate-icon pulse a few seconds in on the very first life so the demo-closer never dead-ends a player who hasn't realized the device itself is the input.
