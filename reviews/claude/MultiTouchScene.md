Level 32 — Multi-touch: hold every screen-space contact pad in a group simultaneously (2, then 3, then 4) to drop the matching gate, then walk Bit through while still holding.
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Numbered gate badges (2/3/4) plus the now-readable 0.2-alpha circuit lines clearly map each plate cluster to its gate, so once the player grasps the trick the "how many fingers" escalation is legible and well-juiced. Reachability is airtight: gates and exit live on one continuous ground with no pits or hazards, so the level is always completable by walking and holding, never climb-bypassable.
CONCERNS: The core conceptual leap (pads are HUD fingers you HOLD, not surfaces Bit walks onto) is never stated until the hint fires, so first-timers may try to platform Bit onto the pads. On iPad the hand-composed climb is purely decorative vertical-fill that the player never needs to ascend, yet it visually invites climbing toward the high plates, and rungs crowd the plate rings in places.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 "ONE OF YOU IS NOT ENOUGH." / "CONTACT REQUIRED" hints at multiplicity without spoiling the finger trick. Escalation exists (notePlayerStruggle on death, whispers at 2/3/4 touches, full reveal in hintText) but is death-gated, and this level has no hazards or pits, so a confused-but-alive player may never trigger it.
SUGGESTION: Add an inactivity/no-progress timer (not just death) to fire notePlayerStruggle, and make the first whisper explicitly say "use more than one finger" so a stuck-but-alive player gets escalating help; on iPad, either gate the climb to actual traversal or visually de-emphasize it so it stops reading as a required platforming route.
