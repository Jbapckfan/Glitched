Level 15 — Low Power toggle swaps normal/lunar gravity per section
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Genuinely clever bidirectional gravity puzzle: the narrow drop is a normal-gravity gate while the wide chasm is a height-gate that only the lunar arc clears, so the player must toggle the POWER button BOTH ways. The iPad path replaces the old left-right course with a confined floor-to-ceiling zig-zag column that fills the frame with no void or overlap, and the apex math is documented and device-independent.
CONCERNS: The on-playfield link between the "POWER ON/OFF" button and gravity is never stated at t=0, and "Low Power" never appears in-scene, so a first-timer has no signal that the bottom-right button flips physics. The bidirectional requirement (OFF for the drop, ON for the chasm) is the hardest idea and gets zero teaching until the struggle-gated hint. On iPhone there is a tall dead-white band between the instruction panel and the gameplay, and the two yellow/green battery pills bottom-left read as ambiguous extra UI next to the POWER button.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 is well-judged and unspoiled: "THE BATTERY IS DYING / EVERYTHING GETS LIGHTER WHEN I LET GO" is atmospheric and never names the button or the toggle trick. Escalation is solid and wired correctly — notePlayerStruggle fires per death, notePlayerProgress resets on the first low-power engage, and hintText spells out both directions of the POWER button precisely once a player is stuck.
SUGGESTION: Add a one-time diegetic cue the first time Bit walks near the POWER button (or on the chasm's first death) such as a small "tap to throttle power" label or a brief gravity-arrow flash, so the button-to-gravity link is discoverable without spoiling the bidirectional reversal, and clarify/remove the two battery pills so the only obvious control is POWER.
