Level 1 — Drag the LEVEL 1 HUD banner down into the spike pit to form a bridge
VERDICT: ship (4/5)
STRENGTHS: Strong teaching level: the 4th-wall conceit (the OS's own title becomes a physical bridge) is legible in both screenshots, and the un-jumpable 240pt pit (well past Bit's ~184pt reach) forces the intended mechanic instead of letting a jump bypass it. iPad re-composes into a full-height serpentine climb that fills the canvas with no void or overlap and stages the drag as a top-of-climb finale.
CONCERNS: On iPhone the cyan t=0 whisper renders low-contrast on the white background and sits in the lower-center band right where Bit spawns, so it competes with the character for attention. The drop hit-test is very forgiving (any central-band drop near the pit bridges), which rescues accessibility/sim fallbacks but lets a player solve it without truly aiming, slightly diluting the "aha."
CLUE/HINT: t=0 is well de-spoiled: a 2s-delayed whisper points at "the sign with my number on it" (the banner) without ever naming the drag gesture. A stuck player gets escalating help via hintText() — "Grab the LEVEL 1 banner and drag it straight down into the spike pit" — but only after notePlayerStruggle fires twice AND 8s elapsed, so it never pre-empts discovery.
SUGGESTION: Boost the iPhone whisper's contrast/placement (move it above the spike pit or give it a dark backing plate) so it reads against the white floor and stops colliding with the spawning Bit; consider a brief one-time wiggle/pulse on the banner the first time the whisper fires to draw the eye to the draggable element without naming the gesture.
