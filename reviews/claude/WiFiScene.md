Level 13 — Toggle WiFi to solidify signal platforms / pass walls
VERDICT: polish (4/5)
STRENGTHS: The two-segment design genuinely forces both states: WiFi-ON stepping stones are the only bridge across the 232-logical chasm, then a too-tall wall plus an OFF-only climb step force exactly one OFF toggle, so the mechanic can't be brute-forced by jumping. The on-screen blue/slashed toggle buttons, signal-bar HUD, download bar, and the wall's antenna texture all reinforce the "signal = solid" read, and toggles only ever happen while Bit stands on a WiFi-independent rest ledge, so nothing de-solidifies underfoot on the intended path. The iPad climb is a real ascending route that fills the canvas with the trap staged high, not a centered phone course in dead sky.
CONCERNS: Visual legibility of the load-bearing pieces is weak: the WiFi-ON stepping stones render as barely-visible 0.3-alpha ghosts and the OFF-step is a faint gray outline, so on first encounter a player can't tell the stones are platforms vs decoration. The WiFi-OFF step's antenna+slash icon is tiny and easy to miss, so the second half ("go OFF to climb") is far less self-evident than the first. On iPad the finale trap and its stones sit off-screen to the right until the camera scrolls, so the signature puzzle isn't previewed. Jump-reach math checks out (all sub-hops and the +55/+45 stairs stay within the ~91pt apex and gap<=130 limits).
CLUE/HINT: t=0 is good: "THE SIGNAL COMES AND GOES..." plus the narrator asides name the theme without revealing the ON-to-cross / OFF-to-climb sequence. But escalation is thin: the only fallback is a single static hintText ("Toggle WiFi in Control Center"), there's no notePlayerStruggle hook, and the hint points to Control Center even though on-screen toggle buttons exist, which can mislead a stuck player.
SUGGESTION: Make the state-dependent surfaces unmistakable: render WiFi-ON stones and the OFF-step at ~0.55-0.6 alpha with a dashed/ghosted fill (not 0.3 near-invisible) and enlarge their icons, and add an escalating hint via notePlayerStruggle (e.g. after N deaths on the chasm: "Signal builds the bridge"; after stalling at the wall: "Drop the signal to climb past the wall") instead of the single Control-Center line.
