Level 18 — Peek the app switcher (swipe up to background/peek) to freeze hazards and reveal dotted spike trajectories, giving you time to plan the jump.
VERDICT: rework (3/5)
STRENGTHS: Strong concept that fuses the fourth-wall narrator ("DON'T YOU DARE SWITCH") with a genuinely un-eye-timeable hazard (desynced, per-leg jitter oscillation) so the freeze is mechanically necessary, not cosmetic. Trajectory lines + physicsWorld.speed=0 + decaying peek budget (5.0->1.5s) is a clean, fair loop, and the phone layout reads cleanly with safe ~25pt gaps / 30pt rises.
CONCERNS: The iPad layout is broken: the diagonal staircase climbs from bottom-left to top-right but the camera follows X only with Y pinned to scene center, so the spawn/start and the instruction panel sit in different vertical zones, the top tiers (and the exit) scroll into screen with huge dead whitespace, and the spike hover offsets (80-140pt above each beat) put the finale crossfire far above its platform. The trajectory lines are drawn as scene children (addChild) while hazards/platforms also live in the scene but the camera scrolls, so on iPad lines render correctly only because everything is scene-space, yet the freeze overlay/timer are camera-parented at center, leaving the timer visually detached from the action low on screen.
CLUE/HINT: t=0 panel ("THE WORLD HOLDS ITS BREATH WHEN YOU LOOK AWAY / SO. STOP WATCHING.") nicely hints "look away = freeze" without naming the App Switcher, so it teases without spoiling. Escalation exists: handleDeath calls notePlayerStruggle and hintText() is explicit ("Swipe up slightly... the spikes' paths draw themselves"), and enterPeekMode calls notePlayerProgress to reset the timer on success.
SUGGESTION: For iPad, drop the diagonal-climb redesign and instead scale/center the proven phone course as a single horizontal band (like the phone path's courseX centering) at the vertical mid-screen, OR add vertical camera-follow that tracks Bit's Y so the climb, exit, and freeze timer stay co-located. The current X-only camera over a full-height diagonal is the root cause of the void/detached-HUD issues.
