Level 30 — Climb a vertical zigzag ladder of bright credit "platforms" to a star exit door, dodging scurrying bug hazards; the credits roll is a deliberate fake-out ending before World 5 rips it back.
VERDICT: polish (3/5)
STRENGTHS: The fake-out concept is genuinely charming and the platforms-as-credits gag reads instantly. Jump-reach is provably safe on both devices: phone rise 76 < 85 cap and zigzag center gaps of 60 < 130; iPad rises are verticalTier-clamped to <=85 and halfSpread is capped at 120 < 130, so every hop is reachable. Bugs got a white-fill legibility pass so they actually read as hazards against the black backdrop.
CONCERNS: t=0 instruction-panel overlap is the headline bug. On iPhone the camera-anchored panel collides with the "LEVEL 30" title's "30" glyph and its right edge clips the pause-button chevron. On iPad it's worse: the centered panel sits on the top finale beat, with "FOR PLAYING" peeking above it and a "YOU" credit box bleeding out below, and the star exit door floats just over it -- the very top of the climb is half-hidden behind the HUD card. On both devices the astronaut spawns overlapping the "GLITCHED PRODUCTION / THE FINAL LEVEL" boxes, mangling that text.
CLUE/HINT: Excellent. The t=0 panel ("CLIMB THE CREDITS / WATCH OUT FOR BUGS") frames it as an ordinary level and never tips the fake-out, preserving the twist. Escalation is wired: handleDeath() calls notePlayerStruggle() and hintText() returns "Climb the credits -- watch for bugs!" -- same non-spoiler info on repeat death.
SUGGESTION: Fix the t=0 panel placement: on iPad the centered panel must not land on the finale tier/exit door -- offset it into a genuinely empty band (or move it to the lower spawn area near Bit), and on iPhone push it clear of both the "LEVEL 30" title row and the pause column. Separately, nudge Bit's spawn 20-30pt so it doesn't overlap the TEACH platform's text. Both are pure layout offsets with no mechanic risk.
