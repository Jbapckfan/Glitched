Level 33 — App Store Review
The optional review prompt as a meta-gag is clever, but first-encounter fairness is undermined by the instruction panel's "THE FINAL LEVEL / HOW HARD CAN IT BE?" line—players may not realize the exit is locked behind a joke gate. The completability math is sound: the two joke gates (INSERT COIN / PREMIUM CONTENT) auto-open after their comedic sequences, and the exit unlocks after 10 seconds of drama regardless of whether the player taps the review button. The hint quality is good: hintText() ("The game wants something from you...") is appropriately ambiguous, and the review button's accessibility label ("optional. The exit unlocks either way.") is a masterstroke. A specific code bug: inLevelReviewButtonUsed is set when the button is tapped, but the 10-second fallback (reviewUnlockFallback) runs regardless—if the player taps the button, the fallback should be removed to avoid a double unlock. One concrete improvement: add a small "?" icon on the exit door before the review sequence starts, hinting that there's a puzzle about the exit, not just a gate.
