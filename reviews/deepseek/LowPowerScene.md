Level 15 — Low Power Mode
The low-power mechanic is clearly telegraphed by the battery HUD and the visual degradation of platforms when active, but first-encounter fairness suffers because the POWER button (the manual fallback) is easy to miss in the bottom-right corner—players may spend their first few attempts wondering why the float feels different. The jump-reach math is sound: the height-gate shelf sits 128.5pt above the normal gravity apex and 36.3pt below the low-gravity apex, making the mechanic genuinely required on every device. The instruction panel's atmospheric lines avoid spoiling the trick at t=0, and the progressive hint system appropriately escalates through notePlayerStruggle() to surface the explicit POWER-button reveal after repeated deaths. A specific rough edge: the iPad composed climb's beatTiers sequence can produce overlapping adjacent platforms at the same tier when the band is short, creating a visually confusing "flat run" that undermines the intended zig-zag rhythm. One concrete improvement: add a small pulsing glow or "tap to toggle" indicator to the POWER button during the first 10 seconds of play, so players discover the manual fallback without needing to die repeatedly first.
