Level 10 — Orientation
The crusher-chase with orientation-based corridor widening is a masterclass in forced discovery, but first-encounter fairness is aggressive—the crusher's first-life grace period (5 seconds at 10% speed) gives the player time to read, but the explicit "rotate" instruction has been replaced by the atmospheric "UP IS A MATTER OF OPINION" line, which may not be clear enough. The completability math is sound: the corridor gap is 18pt in portrait (impossible) and 100pt in landscape (comfortable), and the crusher creeps at 8pt/s after grace, so the player must rotate to survive. The hint quality is good: hintText() explicitly names the rotate gesture, and the discovery panel's "UP IS A MATTER OF OPINION" is perfectly ambiguous. A specific code bug: groundContacts is incremented for every ground body (floor + 2 corridor walls), but didEnd decrements it on each contact—if a wall contact ends while Bit is still on the floor, groundContacts can drop to 0 incorrectly, making Bit airborne while standing on the floor. One concrete improvement: track each ground body separately using a Set<SKNode> rather than a single integer, so Bit remains grounded as long as any ground body is still in contact.
