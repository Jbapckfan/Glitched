Level 32 — Multi-Touch Pressure
The multi-touch pressure plates are a clever physical challenge, but first-encounter fairness is weak on iPhones—the plates are spaced far apart, and players with small hands may struggle to hold 4 plates simultaneously. The completability math is sound: gates are un-jumpable blockers (16pt wide, 100pt tall), and groups require exactly 2/3/4 plates active, so the mechanic is genuinely required. The hint quality is good: hintText() explicitly tells players to "hold every contact pad," and the plate count labels on gates tell players how many fingers they need. A specific code bug: trackedTouches stores UITouch objects directly, but if a touch ends and is immediately re-began, the new touch has a different pointer, so the old plate won't deactivate correctly—this can leave a plate stuck active. One concrete improvement: add a "ghost ring" that expands outward from each plate when it's active, showing the player that their finger is registered, and shrink the ring when the finger lifts, providing immediate visual feedback.
