Level 18 — App Switcher Peek
The freeze-on-peek mechanic is conceptually strong, but first-encounter fairness is poor because the instruction panel's line "THE WORLD HOLDS ITS BREATH WHEN YOU LOOK AWAY" doesn't tell the player how to trigger the freeze—most players won't intuitively swipe up for the app switcher. The completability math is sound: the spike hazards oscillate with randomized phases and trajectory lines that draw during the freeze, making the peek the only reliable way to read safe paths. The notePlayerProgress() call on entering peek mode correctly resets the hint timer, and the explicit hintText() reveals the swipe gesture after repeated deaths. A specific code bug: the removeTrajectoryLines() method is called on every exit from peek mode, but if the scene transitions while lines are active, they can persist as orphaned nodes in the new scene. One concrete improvement: add a large, translucent "SWIPE UP TO PEEK" text overlay that pulses for the first 5 seconds of play, disappearing after the first successful peek—this teaches the mechanic without spoiling the entire puzzle.
