Level 16 — Shake to Undo
The rotten-platform trap is a brilliant design that genuinely forces the player to engage with the undo mechanic, but the first-encounter fairness is undermined by the instruction panel's three atmospheric lines—they hint at regret and mistakes without telling the player how to undo. The completability math is sound: the catch ledge sits 128pt below the exit body bottom, well above Bit's ~91pt jump apex, so the trap genuinely soft-locks you until you shake. The just-in-time "SHAKE TO UNDO" prompt that appears after the platform collapses is the right kind of escalation, and notePlayerStruggle() correctly resets the hint timer when progress is made. A specific code bug: the positionHistory buffer can grow unbounded on devices with high frame rates if the player remains on the same platform for extended periods, though the time-based trimming prevents the worst case. One concrete improvement: cap positionHistory to the last 300 entries regardless of time, and add a visual "rewind trail" ghost effect that slowly fades in behind the player as they approach the rotten platform, telegraphing the undo mechanic more organically.
