Level 20 — Delete to Win
The simulated purge mechanic is a masterful fourth-wall break, but first-encounter fairness is severely compromised by the instruction panel's "IT WANTS YOU TO TURN BACK" line—players may interpret this as a warning to avoid the wall, not a directive to push into it. The completability math works: the corruption wall's physics blocker contacts Bit when he walks into it, and the proximity fallback (>=0.6) ensures even players who don't touch the exact center trigger the purge. The hint quality is strong: hintText() explicitly tells players to "hold toward the wall," and the fake crash screen after walking into the wall is unmistakable. A specific code bug: hasShownFakeReview is set to true at the start of beginSimulatedPurge(), but if the player walks away and returns, the purge won't re-trigger—this is intentional but could confuse players who didn't see the crash screen the first time. One concrete improvement: add a subtle "push" arrow or direction indicator on the corruption wall that pulses when Bit is within 50pt of it, visually cueing the player to walk into it rather than avoid it.
