===== REVIEW: LowPowerScene =====
Level 15 — Low Power Mode
The UI toggle and dying battery aesthetic set up the lunar physics well, making the first encounter with the mechanic clear and fair. The wide chasm gap (125pt) paired with a 200pt shelf correctly height-gates the exit, making the float mandatory and completable within the physics envelope on all devices. The initial text gives atmospheric framing ("let go") without spoiling the trick, and repeated struggle earns the direct hint text smoothly. However, the degradePlatformVisuals method permanently alters the alpha and lineWidth properties of every second platform's SKShapeNode, meaning that toggling low power off and on again will repeatedly stack or fail to cleanly restore the original visual states. A concrete improvement would be to store the original stroke width and alpha values of the platforms during initialization, ensuring they can be perfectly restored when the player toggles normal gravity back on.

===== REVIEW: ShakeUndoScene =====
Level 16 — Shake to Undo
The visual clock motif and the unmissable "rotten platform" trap clearly communicate that mistakes are reversible, offering a brilliant introduction to the mechanic. The confined zigzag column and pre-trap anchor ensure that gaps and rises remain well within the 130pt/85pt limits on both the iPad and iPhone layouts. The clue panel "REGRET HAS A GRIP" avoids early spoilers, while the direct prompt escalates perfectly if the player is stranded on the catch ledge. A notable rough edge is that the rewind logic animates the moving platform to its target position over 0.2 seconds (movingPlatform.run(.move(to: targetPlatformPos...))) instead of snapping it instantly, which can cause severe physics desyncs if the player attempts to jump onto it mid-rewind. A concrete improvement is to immediately snap the moving platform's position to its historical coordinate during the rewind execution to prevent any collision artifacts.

===== REVIEW: AirplaneModeScene =====
Level 17 — Airplane Mode
The airplane mode requirement is introduced fairly, keeping the flying platforms buried safely in the death zone until the player activates the mechanic. Jump reaches are meticulously maintained on both paths; the iPad layout's 125pt horizontal steps and single-tier vertical rises conform perfectly to Bit's capabilities. The initial panel "CUT THE WORLD LOOSE" offers an elegant thematic clue, while successive deaths surface the explicit Control Center instruction seamlessly. A minor bug occurs in updateAirplaneState, where the fallback protection prevents a grounded player from dropping when the mode goes offline, but it relies on an exact isGrounded state that might flicker to false if the player is micro-bouncing or moving between platform seams during a background network blip. A concrete improvement would be to add a short grace period or check the player's downward velocity to ensure they aren't unfairly dropped if they happen to be slightly airborne when the connection drops.

===== REVIEW: AppSwitcherScene =====
Level 18 — App Switcher Peek
The freeze effect paired with the drawing of trajectory lines communicates the app switcher peek excellently without unfairly killing the player on their first glance. The zigzag column cleanly structures the iPad path, keeping rises clamped to the safe maxJumpableRise and edge-to-edge gaps well below 130pt. The atmospheric "THE WORLD HOLDS ITS BREATH" avoids early spoilers, gracefully giving way to the literal swipe-up hint upon repeated player failure. However, the trajectory line drawing creates many small, overlapping line segments using a while loop inside showTrajectoryLines, which is computationally expensive and can cause frame drops if recreated frequently during rapid peeks. A concrete improvement is to replace the loop-based dashed trajectory generation with a single SKShapeNode path using CGPath(dashingWithPhase:lengths:) for significantly better rendering performance.

===== REVIEW: FaceIDScene =====
Level 19 — Face ID Gate
The multi-step scanning process and the doppelganger race provide a brilliant twist on identity verification that feels both fair and highly engaging. The vertical tier spacing keeps platform ascents under 85pt, while the un-jumpable 150pt blocker properly gates progression to ensure the puzzle cannot be bypassed. Initial hints encourage scanning without spoiling the fake-door twist, and failure gracefully scales up to full disclosure of the solution. A rough edge exists in the resolveFallbackName() method; while it safely extracts the owner's name, if UIDevice.current.name uses a smart quote (e.g., ') instead of a straight apostrophe, the method fails to trim the possessive, displaying awkward text like "Usuario's". A concrete improvement is to update the fallback name extraction to check for both straight and smart quotes (or use a regular expression) to ensure the display name is consistently clean.

===== REVIEW: MetaFinaleScene =====
Level 20 — Delete to Win
Presenting the level as a corrupted, impassable wall alongside a simulated app-delete purge is a striking, fair fourth-wall break that fits the meta theme perfectly. ===== REVIEW: VoiceCommandScene =====

===== REVIEW: OrientationScene =====
Level 10 — Orientation
Widening the corridor by physically rotating the device to landscape is a straightforward yet incredibly satisfying "aha" moment.
