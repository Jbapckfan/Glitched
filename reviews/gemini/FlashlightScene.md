Level 31 — Flashlight
Utilizing the real device flashlight and tilt to manipulate a light cone is a highly immersive, perfectly executed hardware puzzle. The vertical tier spacing keeps the iPad ascent within 85pt jumps, while the iPhone's flat cavern maintains edge-to-edge gaps comfortably under 130pt. The initial "IT'S DARK DOWN HERE" clue and the pulsing battery icon provide excellent subtle nudges before the explicit tilt-to-aim instructions drop. A rough edge in the code is that updateLightCone calculates target dimensions based on currentPitch, but currentPitch is only updated via the .flashlightAngleChanged event, which may not fire frequently enough during rapid orientation shifts, causing the light cone to visibly stutter. A concrete improvement is to continually poll the device's pitch from the DeviceManagerCoordinator inside updatePlaying rather than relying solely on discrete event updates.
