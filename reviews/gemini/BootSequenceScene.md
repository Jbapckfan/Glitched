Level 0 — BOOT
The Boot Sequence scene effectively introduces the game's interactive elements with its draggable progress bar mechanic. The immediate visual hint "DRAG TO COMPLETE →" ensures clarity, making the puzzle fair for first-time players. As a non-platforming tutorial, jump-reach concerns are irrelevant here. The scene avoids spoiling any future mechanics, and the engaging fourth-wall commentary about the operator's time adds charm without acting as a hint for a stuck player. However, the reliance on Implicitly Unwrapped Optionals for `progressHandle` and `cursorNode`, while mitigated by guard statements, introduces potential for nil-related issues if initialization order is ever altered. The `fontColor` logic in `addBootLine` is also slightly verbose for its intended outcome.

One concrete improvement would be to refactor `progressHandle` and `cursorNode` to be standard optionals, explicitly unwrapping them when they are guaranteed to be present, or initializing them earlier in the scene's lifecycle to eliminate the need for IUOs.
