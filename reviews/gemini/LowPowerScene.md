Level 15 — Low Power Mode
The UI toggle and dying battery aesthetic set up the lunar physics well, making the first encounter with the mechanic clear and fair. The wide chasm gap (125pt) paired with a 200pt shelf correctly height-gates the exit, making the float mandatory and completable within the physics envelope on all devices. The initial text gives atmospheric framing ("let go") without spoiling the trick, and repeated struggle earns the direct hint text smoothly. However, the degradePlatformVisuals method permanently alters the alpha and lineWidth properties of every second platform's SKShapeNode, meaning that toggling low power off and on again will repeatedly stack or fail to cleanly restore the original visual states. A concrete improvement would be to store the original stroke width and alpha values of the platforms during initialization, ensuring they can be perfectly restored when the player toggles normal gravity back on.
