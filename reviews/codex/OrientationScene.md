Level 10 — Device rotation (portrait vs. landscape)

The orientation trick is fair because the corridor is visibly too tight and the crusher gives a failure case that matches "UP IS A MATTER OF OPINION." There are no meaningful platform gaps; the puzzle is about rotating the device so the player fits through a horizontal constraint. Deaths call `notePlayerStruggle()`, and the hint names rotating to landscape, so the escalation is appropriate. The world offset and player world-width changes look like deliberate fixes for clamp-related softlocks. A rough edge is the amount of manually cached portrait/iPad framing state, which could become stale across safe-area or resize changes. One concrete improvement: add a nonfatal squeeze animation or warning at the corridor before the first crusher death, so the player gets one readable failure without losing a life.
