Level 32 — Multiple simultaneous finger presses

The opening "ONE OF YOU IS NOT ENOUGH" is a good clue, and the glowing grouped plates make the multi-finger rule visually fair. There are essentially no dangerous platform gaps on the main route, and the iPad climb is intentionally single-screen with documented safe rises and gaps. Deaths call `notePlayerStruggle()`, and the hint eventually names the two/three/four-finger hold requirements. The mechanic is still demanding because one touch must often drive Bit while other fingers keep plates pressed, but that is the point of the level. The rough edge is progress wiring: opening a gate calls `resetProgressTimer()` instead of `notePlayerProgress()`, so the shared hint state may not reset as cleanly as other scenes. One concrete improvement: call `notePlayerProgress()` whenever a group opens, and make fallback multi-touch events map to visible plate holds rather than silently activating the next group.
