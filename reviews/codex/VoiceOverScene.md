Level 27 — VoiceOver accessibility feature

The concept is fair because the path is invisible/intangible until VoiceOver or the fallback phases it in, and real versus barred stones give a readable post-reveal rule. The jump route is carefully budgeted, including iPhone stone counts and an iPad one-tier climb with safe edge gaps. Deaths call `notePlayerStruggle()`, and the scene adds its own death-count nudges before and after the path is phased in. The t=0 clue is atmospheric, but "named aloud" can imply speech rather than VoiceOver specifically, so some players may not connect it to accessibility settings. The biggest design compromise is intentional but still important: `forceHardwareFallback(for: .voiceOver)` is called at setup, making the on-screen fallback the default path and making the real system toggle optional. One concrete improvement: present the fallback after one or two failed attempts, while still warning that real VoiceOver should be turned back off before platforming.
