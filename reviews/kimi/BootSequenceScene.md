Level 0 — BOOT

The boot log at line 112 blurts out “DRAG TO 100% TO BOOT” and the on-screen hint at line 363 reads “DRAG TO COMPLETE →” immediately, so the first encounter is hand-holding rather than discovery. As a non-platforming onboarding level, the 130/85 jump-reach budgets do not apply, but the level is still completable only because the answer is broadcast from the start. That spoon-feeding masks any underlying reach or timing issues instead of validating that the interaction itself is fair. The hint pipeline is underdeveloped: there is no override of `notePlayerStruggle()` or escalating `hintText()` chain, so a stuck player only receives the base-class no-progress hint after ~18–22s. Code rough edges include the force-unwrap of `[UIColor.cyan, .magenta, .yellow].randomElement()!` at line 263 and redundant completion marking when `completeBootSequence()` calls `succeedLevel()` at line 577 and then `markCompleted(levelID)` again at line 730. `handleTutorialTouchMoved` also swallows all `moved` events at line 718 before the player has actually dragged. One concrete improvement is to replace the upfront spoiler text with a neutral prompt and add a level-specific `notePlayerStruggle()` override that escalates from “drag the circle” to directional hints after 3–5 seconds of idle tapping.
