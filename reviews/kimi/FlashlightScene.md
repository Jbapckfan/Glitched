Level 31 — Flashlight

The t=0 HUD tease (“IT’S DARK DOWN HERE”) and the flashlight/tilt icons set up the mechanic without spoiling it, and the ambient glow plus exit-glow fallback keep first-time players from bricking if the sensor never fires. Platforming stays within the safe budget on both routes: iPhone gaps peak around 60 pt, the iPad staircase uses verticalTier’s 85 pt clamp, and the widest edge-to-edge gap is about 110 pt between beats 11 and 12—still under the 130 pt limit. The hint ladder, however, is all-or-nothing: notePlayerStruggle() at line 1714 only increments the base struggleCount, and the first triggered hintText() at line 1756 immediately dumps the full solution, with no escalating nudges between the base 18–22 s timer and the complete reveal. Several code rough edges undermine the polish: instructionPanel is force-unwrapped on line 1324, the stalactiteNodes and floorTrapMarkers arrays declared on lines 53–54 are appended on lines 1066 and 1162 but never read, and the isWideCanvas threshold is hardcoded to 700 pt on line 107, silently falling back to the flat iPhone layout in iPad Split View. Worse, tierNear(_:) at lines 516–523 uses strict < tie-breaking, so the stalactite at x=2630—equidistant from beats 12 and 13—snaps to the earlier, lower tier rather than the one the player is jumping toward. A concrete improvement is to override notePlayerStruggle() and use struggleCount to gate progressive lines: flashlight nudge, upright-aim hint, flat-for-pits hint, and only then the full hintText() reveal.
