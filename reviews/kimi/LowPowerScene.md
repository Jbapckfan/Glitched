Level 15 — Low Power

The gravity-swap concept is readable, but the in-scene POWER button is the dominant interaction, so most players will solve the level without ever needing the real Low Power Mode toggle and the device-feature premise is undercut. Platforming margins are technically within budget—the 125-pt finale chasm stays just under the 130-pt horizontal cap (line 427) and the tiered climb clamps each step to `maxJumpableRise` (lines 319-321)—but the 220-pt catch-to-shelf rise (lines 236, 307-308) is intentionally a gravity gate, and the whole finale depends on the assumption that jumps launch at the 620 dy cap even though `BitCharacter` applies a 470 impulse and only caps at 620 (lines 848-853). The t=0 text ("THE BATTERY IS DYING.", line 664) is atmospheric and avoids spoiling, yet the hint system is binary: `notePlayerStruggle()` on every death (line 871) eventually surfaces the one-shot `hintText()` (lines 886-887), which immediately reveals the exact button location and the normal/low toggle sequence. Code quality slips with multiple force-unwraps (`bit: BitCharacter!`, `playerController: PlayerController!`, `batteryIndicator: SKNode!` at lines 12-13 and 29), dead scaffolding because `composedCourseExtent` is hardcoded to 0 (lines 487, 722), and the manual toggle is always visible despite being described as a simulator fallback (line 559). The apex comments also rely on an unexplained `* 150` divisor (lines 178, 289) that is never validated against the actual physics simulation. One concrete fix: gate the manual POWER button behind `ProcessInfo.isLowPowerModeEnabled` availability so it only appears when real-device Low Power Mode is undetectable, and replace the instant full spoiler with graduated hints that first call attention to the battery HUD color change, then the lower-right control, and only finally disclose the precise toggle timing.
