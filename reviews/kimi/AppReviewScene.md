The gag is clear from the start, but first-encounter fairness hinges on whether the player trusts the padlock fallback (lines 1013–1018) rather than feeling forced to tap the coercive "VALIDATE ME" button (line 969). Completability is not a platforming concern: the ground is flat and continuous (lines 190–191), so edge-to-edge gap and top-to-top rise budgets are irrelevant, yet the level does nothing to show the button is optional until the lock expires. The t=0 text "JUST GET TO THE EXIT. HOW HARD CAN IT BE?" (lines 514–526) frames the joke without outright spoiling the wait-it-out solution, but after that the help dries up. `hintText()` at line 1422 falls back on the vague "The game wants something from you..." and there is no `notePlayerStruggle()` override, so struggling players only get the base-class 18–22s nudge rather than escalating reassurance. The single most valuable fix is to add a `notePlayerStruggle()` override that explicitly tells the player the lock breaks on its own, turning the ending from confusion into generosity.
