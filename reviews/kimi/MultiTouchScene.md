The opening subtitle "CONTACT REQUIRED" and the t=0 line "ONE OF YOU IS NOT ENOUGH" (line 846) imply multiple fingers without spelling out the input, and the gate lock badges show the required contact count, so the first encounter is readable. On iPhone the path is flat walking, and on iPad the per-tier rise is clamped to `BaseLevelScene.maxJumpableRise` at line 419 with X-advance capped at 96 pt, so the advertised ≤130 gap and ≤85 rise budgets hold; however, the climb is purely decorative because gates 1–3 and the exit all stay on the continuous ground (lines 579–580, 604–605, 642–661), meaning a missed jump only wastes time and platforming never gates progress. Stuck players get only the base-class hint and the single, exhaustive `hintText()` at line 1335; there are no escalating `notePlayerStruggle()` nudges (line 1302 merely triggers the generic base-class behavior), so struggling players are either stuck or fully spoiled. Code rough edges include the `isWideCanvas` heuristic using magic-number thresholds at line 78 (`size.height > 1000 && size.width > 700`), which can misclassify an 11-inch iPad in landscape and drop it to the phone layout, and the `handleGameInput(.multiTouch)` fallback at lines 912–943, where `activateNextFallbackGroup()` can open every gate without the player ever touching a pressure plate. A smaller but real issue is at lines 877–880: `movementTouch` is assigned to the first non-plate touch and never reassigns, so a player who starts moving with a thumb can run out of usable digits for the four-plate finale. One concrete improvement: restrict the multi-touch fallback to simulator/debug builds only, and replace the single hint with escalating struggle hints (e.g., "Try holding two glowing pads at once," then "Keep them held while you walk Bit through") so first-time players get scaffolding without trivializing the puzzle.
