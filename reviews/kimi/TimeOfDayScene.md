Level 25 — Time of Day

The time-of-day mechanic is introduced fairly: the opening text hints the world changes without explicitly naming CYCLE TIME, and the DAY/NIGHT readout plus guard "zzz" animations make the first encounter readable. Platforming stays within safe bounds, with the phone P1→P2 gap at 130 pt edge-to-edge and a 12.5 pt rise, while the iPad path keeps horizontal gaps around 70 pt and rises one tier per hop, clearing the 130/85 budget. Hints are only half-solved: `hintText()` at line 1017 fires only after `notePlayerStruggle()` on death, so a living player hesitating before the guard receives no escalating help, though the t=0 text avoids outright spoilers. Code rough edges are numerous: `cycleTimeOverride()` force-unwraps `overrideMode!` at line 910, `setupBackground()` hardcodes five clock icons at fixed x positions up to 560 (lines 99–105) that scroll off-screen on iPad, `showFourthWall()` spams `GlitchedNarrator.present(...)` on every CYCLE TIME tap (lines 884–894), and contradictory comments at lines 603 and 609 claim instruction widths of 300 and 340 while the code uses 340. The `isWideCanvas` gate at line 56 (`size.height > 1000 && size.width > designWidth + 200`) is another arbitrary hardcoded threshold. One concrete improvement: throttle `showFourthWall()` to once per level entry and wire the existing `hintText()` into a mid-level stall timer so alive, hesitant players get targeted help.
