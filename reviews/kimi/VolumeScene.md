Level 4 — Volume

The t=0 instruction panel (lines 1212–1228) frames the level with “KEEP IT QUIET” and “THE WATER LISTENS,” which avoids an explicit button tell, but the wolf immediately cycles to “zzz… lower the volume… please… zzz” at line 144, spoiling the discovery before the player has experimented; `hintText()` then hands over the exact “Press your device’s Volume-Down button” answer at line 1567, so the first-death hint is a full reveal rather than a graduated nudge. iPhone completability is trivial—a flat floor with the exit at `size.width - 70 * visualScale` (line 508)—so jump-reach is never exercised. The iPad path is unreachable because `isWideCanvas` is hardcoded to `false` at line 76, leaving `buildComposedIPadLevel()`, the tier-count loop, and the lateral-step budget as dead code; if it were enabled, `lateralStepBudget = min(maxJumpableGap, 120)` at line 578 and the `ipadTierCount` growth condition at lines 560–565 at least attempt to keep edge-to-edge gaps within 130 and top-to-top rises within 85. Struggle support is flat: `notePlayerStruggle()` is called only from `handleDeath` (line 1543), and the level offers a single static `hintText()` string (line 1567) with no context-sensitive escalation (e.g., “too loud” vs. “the water is rising”), so a stuck-but-alive player receives no incremental guidance. The water hazard threshold is muddled: the comment declares “Flood!” above 0.7, but `waterHazardActive` is set only when `currentVolume > 0.85` at line 287, meaning players can stand in visibly high water between 0.7 and 0.85 without drowning. The KVO observer at lines 1266–1271 updates the creature and indicator but skips `updateWaterLevel()`, relying on `InputEventBus.shared.post(.volumeChanged(...))` reaching `handleGameInput`; if the bus fires before the scene becomes the input delegate, the flood visual stays out of sync until the next volume change. The most concrete improvement is to remove or `#if DEBUG`-gate the dead iPad composition code and replace the static hint with a real `notePlayerStruggle()` ladder—start with a vague quiet cue, follow with a water-level cue, and delay the explicit Volume-Down reveal until the second or third struggle.
