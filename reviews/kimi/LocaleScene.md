Level 26 — Locale

The locale mechanic is readable from the scrambled sign glyphs and the atmospheric panel, but printing the exact Settings > General > Language revert steps in three languages at t=0 is a heavy-handed spoiler that undercuts the first encounter. Platforming budgets stay within safe limits on the intended routes—iPhone gaps around 42.5 pt with ~60 pt rises and iPad single-tier rises capped at 85 pt—but the 120 pt iPad switchback span and the 137.5 pt iPhone wrong-route dead-end are not clearly marked as off-limits, so players may waste lives testing them. Hint escalation is weak: `hintText()` at line 867 immediately hands out the full Settings walkthrough, while `notePlayerStruggle()` only counts deaths with no Locale-specific intermediate nudges, meaning the fallback after roughly 18-22 s is the same big reveal. The most serious code bug is the early return at lines 670-672: because any event matching `baselineLanguage` returns immediately, the `else if !changedFromBaseline && isUnscrambled` branch at lines 683-691 is unreachable, and reverting to the load-time language after solving leaves the signs permanently unscrambled. There is also a nil-baseline risk: if `LocaleManager.shared.currentLanguageCode` is nil, `changedFromBaseline` falls back to comparing against "en", which could auto-solve the level on launch for non-English devices. One concrete improvement is to drop the baseline early-return, let `puzzleLatched` drive text-only rescrambling on revert, and add an intermediate struggle hint ("The glyphs are letters in another language") before the full Settings solution.
