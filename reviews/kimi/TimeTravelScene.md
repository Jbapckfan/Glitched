Level 9 — Time Travel

The "GROWTH TIME: 10 YEARS" sign and "0 / 10 YRS" readout telegraph the mechanic, but the t=0 panel's vague "ALL THINGS TAKE THEIR TIME..." (line 1005) withholds the actual action so completely that a first-time player may stand still until the base-class hint fires. On iPhone the flat floor is gapless and the grown-tree ladder's branch-to-branch rises are only 34–48 pt with overlapping X-spans (`branchData`, lines 1371–1378), so the climb is comfortably reachable. On iPad the zig-zag pitch is 122 pt (≤130 pt) and the `verticalTier` step is within the 85 pt rise budget, though the long staircase means a misjump sends Bit back to the base pad. There is no `notePlayerStruggle()` override; only a single static `hintText()` at line 1522 returns "Leave the app for a while — time passes", so stuck players get only the base-class fallback after ~18–22 s with no escalating, context-aware coaching. The composed-iPad course-width math at lines 540–542 collapses `leftMargin` with `let courseLeft = leftMargin - 80 - leftMargin` (= -80), contradicting the "include the left margin as scenery" comment and yielding a narrower `composedWorldWidth` than intended. The tree-stage thresholds in `applyTimePassage` (lines 1061–1070) are duplicated almost verbatim in `handleGameInput` (lines 1418–1427), and force-unwraps of freshly-created optionals (`signNode!` at line 865, `clockDisplay!` at line 910) plus the magic-number `isWideCanvas` threshold at line 48 add further fragility. One concrete improvement: extract the stage-threshold logic into a shared helper, fix the `courseLeft` sign error to include the margin correctly, and add a `notePlayerStruggle()` override that nudges the player after idle time or a death, e.g. "The tree won't grow while you watch it."
