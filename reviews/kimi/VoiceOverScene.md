Level 27 — VoiceOver

The opening plaque, "A PATH EXISTS ONLY ONCE IT'S NAMED ALOUD," is vague enough that first-time players may hunt for a microphone instead of enabling VoiceOver or tapping the hardware fallback, so the mechanic's first encounter is more opaque than it appears. Reach is technically safe—the phone gaps stay near 48 pt and rises near 51 pt, while the iPad climb tops out at roughly 83 pt vertical rises and 54–76 pt gaps, both within the 130/85 budgets—but the long iPad chain still leaves little margin for mistimed jumps. The hint system escalates after deaths via `notePlayerStruggle()` and `hintText()`, yet the t=0 text never identifies the on-screen accessibility fallback, so struggling players can cycle through deaths without realizing the button exists. Code rough edges include the hardcoded wide-canvas threshold `min(size.width, size.height) >= 700` (line 185), `setupBit()` hardcoding `verticalTier(0, of: 14, ...)` independently of `tierCount` (line ~437), `buildComposedIPadLevel()` mislabeling its finale gap as 76 pt when the edge-to-edge gap to the exit is 54 pt (line 411), and the forced `AccessibilityManager.shared.forceHardwareFallback(for: .voiceOver)` never being cleared in `willMove(from:)` or `onLevelSucceeded()` (line ~363). The `decoyPositions` comment also claims decoys are "capped to keep it fair" (line 470), but the helper simply returns one decoy per interior gap with no actual cap. The cleanest fix is to call `AccessibilityManager.shared.clearHardwareFallback(for: .voiceOver)` in both `willMove(from:)` and `onLevelSucceeded()` so the overlay does not leak into later levels, and to rewrite the opening hint to name the fallback button explicitly.
