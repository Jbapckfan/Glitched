# Screenshot and App Preview Plan

Goal: make the novelty obvious in the first two seconds. The App Store page should answer one question immediately: "How can a platformer use my actual phone as the controller?"

Capture principle:
- Use real gameplay footage only.
- Keep captions short and high-contrast.
- Lead with "show a friend" mechanics before lore.
- Avoid finale spoilers in screenshots. Tease the meta narrator without revealing L33.

## 6.7-inch iPhone Screenshot Set

Use 8 screenshots. Capture in portrait. Leave enough top-right clearance for the App Store crop and avoid DEBUG overlays.

1. L8 Dark Mode
   - Moment: split before/after toggle where the whole game recolors.
   - Caption: TOGGLE DARK MODE. THE LEVEL CHANGES.
   - Why first: fastest visual proof that iOS settings are gameplay.

2. L7 Screenshot
   - Moment: player mid-action, screenshot freeze effect active.
   - Caption: TAKE A SCREENSHOT TO FREEZE TIME.
   - Why: immediately viral and easy to demonstrate in video.

3. L9 Orientation
   - Moment: corridor impossible in portrait, widened after device rotation.
   - Caption: ROTATE THE PHONE. RESHAPE THE WORLD.
   - Why: physical movement reads well in stills if paired with rotated-phone overlay.

4. L10 Time Travel
   - Moment: tree grown after leaving and returning to the app.
   - Caption: LEAVE THE APP. COME BACK LATER.
   - Why: shows the game using app lifecycle instead of only sensors.

5. L5 Charging
   - Moment: Bit riding the giant plug upward.
   - Caption: PLUG IN. RIDE THE POWER.
   - Why: weird, physical, and easy to understand.

6. L6 Brightness
   - Moment: UV platforms visible with the brightness bar.
   - Caption: BRIGHTNESS REVEALS THE PATH.
   - Why: communicates continuous device-state puzzles.

7. L20 Meta Finale or L29 The Lie
   - Moment: terminal/meta scene without final spoiler text.
   - Caption: THE OS IS WATCHING.
   - Why: sells the fourth-wall narrator and campaign escalation.

8. L31 Flashlight or L32 Multi-Touch
   - Moment: flashlight cave beam or four pressure nodes active.
   - Caption: EVERY FEATURE IS A WAY OUT.
   - Why: reinforces 34-level breadth and paid-world value.

## App Preview Video, 15-30 Seconds

Recommended length: 24 seconds. Most viewers decide fast; use hard cuts and visible cause/effect.

Storyboard:

0.0-2.0s
- Full-bleed L8 clip: toggle Dark Mode; the world recolors.
- Overlay: YOUR PHONE IS THE CONTROLLER.

2.0-5.0s
- L7: player jumps, screenshot flash, level freezes.
- Overlay: SCREENSHOT = TIME STOP.

5.0-8.0s
- L9: portrait corridor squeezes; phone rotates; corridor opens.
- Overlay: ROTATE TO SOLVE.

8.0-11.0s
- L5: charger plugs in; giant plug lifts Bit.
- Overlay: PLUG IN. GO UP.

11.0-14.0s
- L10: app exits/returns; tree grows.
- Overlay: LEAVE. RETURN. IT GREW.

14.0-17.0s
- L6: brightness slider rises; platforms appear; burn warning briefly teased.
- Overlay: BRIGHTNESS REVEALS.

17.0-20.0s
- Rapid montage: Face ID gate, VoiceOver path, AirDrop keypad, flashlight cave.
- Overlay: 34 LEVELS. 6 WORLDS.

20.0-24.0s
- Bit stands in clean line-art scene; narrator terminal line appears.
- Overlay: GLITCHED
- App Store badge slot.

Audio:
- Use in-game clicks/glitches/haptics where capturable.
- Captions must carry the video with sound off.

Compliance:
- Use only captured gameplay.
- Keep video between 15 and 30 seconds.
- Do not show physical device actions if they imply unsafe behavior. Use hand/phone cutaways only for rotate/plug-in if polished and truthful.
