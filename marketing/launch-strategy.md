# Launch and PR Strategy

## Wedge

Glitched is not "another indie puzzle platformer." The wedge is:

Someone watches a player solve a level by doing something they have never seen a game ask for: changing Dark Mode, taking a screenshot, plugging in the charger, leaving the app, using VoiceOver, or pointing the flashlight.

The marketing line:

"Your phone IS the controller."

The social line:

"This iPhone game made me change my actual phone settings to beat a level."

## Target Audiences

1. iOS puzzle players
   - Fans of Blackbox, Device 6, Monument Valley, The Room, Gorogoa, There Is No Game.
   - Motive: clever mechanics, premium feel, no ads.

2. Short-video viewers
   - TikTok/Reels/Shorts users who respond to "wait for it" mechanic reveals.
   - Motive: novelty and shareability.

3. Apple/device nerds
   - People who enjoy iOS details, weird APIs, settings, haptics, sensors, and App Store oddities.
   - Motive: "I did not know a game could use that."

4. Indie/meta-game fans
   - Fans of fourth-wall games and strange narrator experiences.
   - Motive: the OS is alive and starts pushing back.

5. Accessibility-aware players
   - VoiceOver, fallback controls, and hardware-free paths are real product strengths.
   - Motive: unusual mechanics without making players force-quit when hardware is unavailable.

## TikTok / Reels / Shorts Plan

Format rules:
- 9:16, 12-25 seconds.
- Start with the outcome, then show the mechanic.
- Put the device action in the first 2 seconds.
- Use captions. Assume muted playback.
- Never over-explain. The viewer should feel they discovered the trick.

Content pillars:

1. "The game made me..."
   - "The game made me turn on Dark Mode."
   - "The game made me take a screenshot."
   - "The game made me leave the app for five seconds."
   - "The game made me plug in my charger."

2. "Things iOS games should not know"
   - Brightness level.
   - Low Power Mode.
   - Focus Mode.
   - VoiceOver state.
   - AirDrop/share sheet.

3. Bit/narrator moments
   - Dry terminal lines.
   - "The OS is not happy with you."
   - Meta finale teases without spoilers.

4. Challenge format
   - "Can you solve this without touching the screen?"
   - "What would you do here?"
   - "The door only opens if your phone changes."

First 20 video ideas:

1. Dark Mode level: toggle Settings/Control Center, world flips.
2. Screenshot freeze: take screenshot mid-jump, platform stops.
3. Orientation crusher: rotate phone, corridor opens.
4. Charging elevator: plug cable in, giant plug lifts Bit.
5. Brightness UV: raise brightness, hidden platforms appear, too bright burns.
6. Time Travel: leave app, return, tree grew.
7. Focus Mode: turn on Focus, hazards freeze.
8. Low Power: jump becomes floaty.
9. Face ID gate: scan opens vault.
10. Clipboard: copy the code, door reacts.
11. Notification: tap the correct notification.
12. AirDrop/cipher: share to reveal/decode.
13. Flashlight cave: point the phone to see hazards.
14. Multi-touch: hold multiple nodes, gate opens.
15. VoiceOver: hidden path phases in.
16. App Review finale tease: "It asked for a review. Then it lied."
17. "All free world mechanics in 20 seconds."
18. "Every time the game uses iOS against you."
19. "My friend tried to solve this like a normal platformer."
20. "The OS narrator gets personal."

Posting cadence:
- T-14 to T-7: 1 mechanic reveal per day.
- T-6 to T-1: 2 posts per day, one mechanic and one reaction/challenge.
- Launch day: 4 posts: launch trailer, Dark Mode, screenshot freeze, "World 1 is free."
- Launch week: remix top performer into 3 variants; post comments with App Store link only after engagement starts.

## Product Hunt Angle

Research note:
Product Hunt's own launch guide says makers can hunt their own products and there is no clear advantage to using a third-party hunter: https://www.producthunt.com/launch

Recommended positioning:
- Title: Glitched - an iPhone game where your phone is the controller
- Tagline: Beat levels by using real iOS features
- Gallery: app preview video, Dark Mode GIF, screenshot-freeze GIF, charging/rotate GIF, world map.
- Maker comment:
  - Explain the origin: "What if platformer controls were not buttons, but actual iOS state?"
  - Clarify the business model: World 0+1 free, one Full Game unlock, no ads.
  - Mention accessibility/fallback controls so the gimmick does not sound exclusionary.

Launch day:
- Schedule for early Pacific morning if you can support comments all day.
- Bring your own traffic from short video and indie/iOS communities.
- Do not ask for upvotes. Ask people to try the free first world and comment with the mechanic that surprised them.

## Reddit Angles

r/iosgaming:
- Research note: recent moderation comments indicate Developer Self-Promotion is allowed only on Developer Saturdays, and unrelated self-promotion is removed.
- Post only when the app is live on the App Store.
- Title:
  - "[Dev] I made an iOS puzzle-platformer where Dark Mode, screenshots, charging, and leaving the app are level mechanics"
- Body:
  - Lead with the playable free slice.
  - Include 3 GIFs directly in the post: Dark Mode, screenshot, charging/rotate.
  - Be transparent: "World 0+1 are free; one IAP unlocks the rest; no ads."
  - Ask a genuine question: "Which iOS feature would you expect a level to use?"

r/IndieGaming:
- Use a short GIF-first post; do not lead with store link.
- Angle: "The controller is the phone itself."

r/gamedev:
- Postmortem angle after launch, not launch-day promo.
- Title:
  - "I built 34 iOS levels around real device features. Here is what broke."
- Body:
  - Talk about simulator fallback, App Review, VoiceOver, Dark Mode, brightness, and device-specific QA.
  - This is more HN/gamedev-friendly than a launch ad.

Hacker News:
- Use Show HN only if there is a web landing page and a live App Store link.
- Title:
  - "Show HN: I built an iPhone puzzle game where the device itself is the controller"
- Best angle:
  - Technical implementation story: using iOS state as game input, without trapping players who cannot use a given hardware feature.
- First comment:
  - Explain what APIs/features are involved at a high level.
  - Mention the simulator/accessibility fallback problem as an engineering constraint.

### Product Hunt Maker Comment

Hey Product Hunt. I built Glitched because I wanted an iOS game that could not really exist anywhere else.

It is a line-art puzzle-platformer where the controller is the phone: screenshots, Dark Mode, brightness, charging, Focus, Low Power Mode, Face ID, VoiceOver, AirDrop, flashlight, and other iOS features become level mechanics.

## Launch Week Timeline

T-21 to T-14:
- Finalize App Store page metadata.
- Capture screenshots and trailer.
- Build landing page with press kit links.
- Prepare 20 short-video drafts.
- Assemble press list and creator list.

T-13 to T-7:
- Send embargoed press emails.
- Start posting teaser clips without the store link.
- Ask 5-10 trusted testers to record reaction footage.
- Confirm App Store IAP price and Game Center IDs.

T-6 to T-1:
- Submit Product Hunt page.
- Schedule launch-day posts.
- Prepare Reddit posts but do not post early.
- Send creator follow-ups with trailer/GIFs.

Launch day:
- Release App Store link.
- Post launch trailer.
- Post best mechanic clip.
- Publish Product Hunt.
- Post on r/iosgaming only if the day fits Developer Saturday rules.
- Monitor support, purchase/restore, crash reports, and reviews.

Launch +1 to +7:
- Post daily mechanic clips.
- Share "making of" thread: how iOS settings became puzzles.
- Reply to every serious comment.
- Pitch App Store editorial through App Store Connect if available.
- Publish a devlog/postmortem after first-week data.

## Success Metrics

Primary:
- App Store product-page conversion.
- World 1 completion rate.
- Full Game unlock conversion.
- Trailer watch-through first 5 seconds.

Secondary:
- Short-video saves/shares.
- Reddit comment quality.
- Press replies.
- Product Hunt comments from iOS/puzzle players.

Warning signs:
- Players do not understand that real device features are required.
- Players think the app permanently changes settings.
- Paywall arrives before trust is built.
- Device-feature levels feel inaccessible without discovering fallback controls.
