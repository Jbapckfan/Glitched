# Positioning and Pricing

## One-Liner

Glitched is an iOS puzzle-platformer where your phone is the controller: beat levels by using real device features like Dark Mode, screenshots, charging, Face ID, VoiceOver, AirDrop, and the flashlight.

Shorter:
Your phone IS the controller.

Store-friendly:
A line-art puzzle-platformer solved with real iOS features.

## Positioning Pillars

1. Native to iPhone
   - Not a port.
   - Not a virtual controller.
   - The device itself is part of the puzzle language.

2. Shareable "aha" mechanics
   - Dark Mode recolors the world.
   - Screenshot freezes a level.
   - Plugging in power becomes an elevator.
   - Leaving the app grows the tree.

3. Premium indie trust
   - Free first world.
   - One Full Game unlock.
   - No ads.
   - No consumables.
   - Accessibility/fallback paths for hardware-gated mechanics.

4. Dry OS narrator
   - The game is not only clever mechanics; it has a voice.
   - Bit/the OS makes the phone feel slightly alive.

## Comps and References

1. Blackbox
   - Reference fit: iOS-native puzzle design that asks players to think outside the touchscreen and use sensors/system behaviors.
   - Difference: Glitched is a platformer campaign with a visible character, levels, worlds, bosses, and a narrator.
   - Research reference: Blackbox App Store page and public descriptions emphasize device/system puzzle behavior, "stop touching the screen", haptics, and Apple Design Award positioning: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/id962969578

2. DEVICE 6
   - Reference fit: Apple Design Award-winning iOS game that used the platform itself as part of the narrative/puzzle experience.
   - Difference: Glitched is action-platforming plus device-feature mechanics, not text-map narrative.
   - Research reference: DEVICE 6 App Store page describes an Apple Design Award 2014 winner and platform-native storytelling; current listed US price was $3.99 at research time: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/device-6/id680366065

3. There Is No Game: Wrong Dimension
   - Reference fit: meta-puzzle comedy where the game argues with the player and breaks expectations.
   - Difference: Glitched is quieter, more OS-like, and built specifically around iOS hardware/system state.
   - Research reference: Steam tags and public descriptions position it around point-and-click, puzzle, adventure, comedy, and meta/narration: https://store.steampowered.com/app/1240210/There_Is_No_Game_Wrong_Dimension/

Secondary comps:
- Monument Valley: premium mobile puzzle trust and App Store-friendly visual clarity.
- The Room: paid mobile puzzle value perception.
- Gorogoa: screenshot-worthy puzzle craft.

## Free-to-Paid Conversion Argument

The free slice is unusually strong because World 0+1 contains the easiest-to-explain mechanics:

- L5 Charging
- L6 Brightness
- L7 Screenshot
- L8 Dark Mode
- L9 Orientation
- L10 Time Travel

That means players can experience the actual hook before paying. This reduces the risk of "buy to find out what it is" and makes the paywall feel like an expansion of a proven trick, not a bait-and-switch.

Recommended paywall framing:

"WORLDS 0-1 FREE. WORLDS 2-5 SEALED."

Bullet proof:
- Unlock Worlds 2-5
- 23 more levels
- No ads
- One purchase
- Restore anytime

Avoid:
- "Support development" as the primary argument.
- Multiple IAPs.
- Consumable hint packs.
- Any dev-commentary product in v1.

## Recommended IAP Price

Recommended launch price:
$4.99 USD for the Full Game unlock.

Why:
- The free slice is generous enough to justify a premium unlock.
- 34 total levels gives enough perceived depth for $4.99.
- The game is no-ads, no-consumables, no-subscription.
- The direct comp space supports paid/premium mobile puzzle pricing better than $0.99.
- A $2.99 price may convert more players but risks underselling a carefully built premium indie. A $6.99+ price is harder before reviews/press prove the value.

Pricing ladder:
- Launch: $4.99
- Launch-week sale option: $3.99 for first 7 days if you want urgency.
- Do not go below $2.99 unless early conversion is badly under target.
- Consider $5.99 only after App Store feature/press/reviews.

International:
- Use App Store Connect's price matrix.
- Keep the base country price stable.
- Avoid regional experimentation until after launch-week analytics.

## Store Page Conversion Thesis

The first 3 screenshots and the preview video should not sell "34 levels" first. They should sell:

1. Dark Mode changes the level.
2. Screenshot freezes time.
3. Rotate/leave/plug in to solve.

Then the description and later screenshots sell depth:

4. 34 levels.
5. 6 worlds.
6. Fourth-wall narrator.
7. One Full Game unlock.

## Risks and Mitigations

Risk:
Players think the game requires uncomfortable real-world setup changes.

Mitigation:
Mention accessibility/fallback controls without making them sound like cheats.

Risk:
Players assume the free slice is the whole game.

Mitigation:
World map screenshot and paywall copy should clearly say Worlds 2-5 are included in Full Game.

Risk:
The game is compared too directly to Blackbox.

Mitigation:
Emphasize "puzzle-platformer campaign" and visible character/worlds, not only no-touch puzzles.

Risk:
Meta-game marketing can sound smug.

Mitigation:
Lead with mechanics. Let the narrator tone appear as flavor, not the whole pitch.
